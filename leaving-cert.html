Leaving Cert

Choosing Romanian for the Leaving Certificate Examination

Why choose Romanian for the Leaving Cert?

The possibility to chose Romanian as a subject for an exam with such importance is a great advantage for native Romanian students. The marks of this subjects add up directly to the final, resulting in a higher mark and increasing the possibility to be admitted to the desired college.

The Romanian Language exam is a higher-level exam, but this should not discourage anyone to chose it, as it’s not supposed to be an extremely difficult one for any Romanian speaker. Still some preparation is needed, especially in the speciality area.

The preparation places a special emphasis on some of the specific sounds of the Romanian alphabet, vocabulary and grammar. Students will learn synonyms, antonyms, homonyms, paronyms, archaisms, regionalisms, neologisms, etc. The is long and it can seem impossible to learn all these strange sounding things, but the truth is that with some practice each student will be able to master them.

Where to prepare?

One of the aims of the Romanian Language, Culture, and Civilization course, mostly in the final two years is to prepare students for the Leaving Cert. But the course is not an option is every school where there are Romanian students, since it is not introduced in every school.

For those students who doesn’t have the option to opt for Romanian preparation classes in their school, there are other options to choose from:

  1. If there is a school in your area where the Romanian course is introduced, you could ask the directorate of the school, is as an outsider you can join in. If it’s now possible, you can join the mixed group (students from different schools) that meet at the Teachers’ Club
